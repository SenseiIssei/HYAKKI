# 01 — Fiction & Glossary

## The world, in full

There was a god. It is not important what it was the god *of* — by the time the Myriad reached it, it had stopped being of anything.

Ten thousand soldiers were sent. Not an army: a **myriad**, the old unit, exactly ten thousand, no officers, no names, only numbers stitched into the back of each coat. They marched into the god's territory and they killed it.

The killing worked. The dying did not.

A god's death is not an event, it's a *process*, and this one got stuck. The god began to die and then forgot the next part. So it dies, and dies, and dies, in a loop that has now been going for an unknown duration — the Myriad's own records disagree with each other by centuries.

The corpse is called **the Hollow**. It is enormous and it is still warm and it is still, in the ways that matter, *working*. Its immune system still fires. Its organs still contract. Its memory still runs, which is why the dead soldiers inside it keep getting reissued.

You are one soldier. You march deeper. When you die, the Hollow's memory does what it does to everything inside it: it plays you back from the beginning. You wake at the mouth of the wound with a new number on your coat and the strange, unearned muscle of a body that has done this before.

The Ash you carry is what's left of everything the Hollow burned through you. It's the only thing that survives the replay.

---

## Why the numbers go up forever (the diegetic excuse for infinite scaling)

The Hollow generates defenders *proportionally to what has already killed them*. It's an immune response, and it learns. Rank 1 is an antibody. Rank 400 is an antibody that has studied four hundred ranks of your corpses.

This is stated once, in a fragment, around Reveille 30. After that the player just knows.

## Glossary — the naming layer

Every system has a diegetic name. **Never use the generic term in UI copy.**

### Places & structures
| Term | Means | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **The Hollow** | The world / the god's corpse | Never "the dungeon", never "the world" |
| **The Column** | The main endless run | Your soldier is *in* the Column |
| **The Mouth** | Where every run begins | The wound you march into |
| **A Rank** | One wave / one enemy encounter | "Rank 214" not "Wave 214" |
| **A Stand** | Every 10th Rank — a Warden fight | Timed. "You made your Stand at Rank 90." |
| **A Descent** | One dungeon run | Katabasis. Below the Column. |
| **A Layer** | A dungeon biome | The Ossuary, The Drowned Barracks, etc. |

### Actions & states
| Term | Means | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Reveille** | Prestige tier 1 — soft reset | "Sound Reveille." The wake-up call. |
| **Interment** | Prestige tier 2 — reset the Ash tree | "Be interred." You're buried; something else gets up. |
| **Apotheosis** | Prestige tier 3 — reset everything | "Ascend." The god-becoming. |
| **The Urge** | Optional active-play buff | "Urge the Column forward." |
| **A Vow** | A self-imposed challenge modifier | "Take the Vow of Salt." |
| **The Autopsy** | Post-run summary | Never "Game Over" |

### Currencies
| Term | Tier | Earned from | Spent on |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Bone** | Run-scoped | Killing things in the current run | Temporary run upgrades. Lost on Reveille. |
| **Ash** | Prestige 1 | Reveille, scaled by deepest Rank | The permanent tree (Flesh / Iron / Rite) |
| **Names** | Prestige 2 | Interment, Wardens, Descents | Class unlocks, relic slots, Vow slots |
| **Ichor** | Prestige 3 | Apotheosis | Endgame systems, reality-level modifiers |
| **Keys** | Regenerating | Time (1 per 20 min, capped) | Entering a Descent |

### People & things
| Term | Means |
|---|---|
| **The Myriad** | The original ten thousand. Also, eventually, you. |
| **The Returned** | Dead soldiers the Hollow reissued. Includes snapshots of your own past runs. |
| **A Warden** | One of ten named recurring bosses. Each evolves across prestige layers. |
| **Chaff** | Basic enemies. Immune-system antibodies. |
| **Organs** | Elite enemies. Parts of the god still functioning. |
| **Nothing** | Endgame enemies past Rank ~500. Negative space. They erase rather than damage. |
| **A Relic** | Equipment. Always singular objects with histories, never "gear". |
| **A Fragment** | One piece of unlockable lore text. |

## Voice guide for all in-game copy

**Rules:**
1. Short sentences. Frequently incomplete.
2. Second person, present tense, for anything happening now.
3. Passive voice is *allowed and encouraged* for things the Hollow does to you.
4. Never explain a mechanic in lore voice and never explain lore in mechanic voice. Tooltips are clinical. Fragments are not.
5. Numbers are sacred; when a fragment mentions a number, spell out the impossible ones. *"Ten thousand."* Not *"10,000."*
6. Never use exclamation marks. Not once, anywhere in the game.

**Examples — good:**
- *"You wake at the Mouth. Your coat says 47. You do not remember 46."*
- *"Rank 214 held. The thing in it did not."*
- *"While you slept: seven hours. Forty-one deaths. Something kept the count for you."*
- *"It has your gait."*

**Examples — bad:**
- ~~"Congratulations! You've prestiged!"~~
- ~~"The evil god must be destroyed!"~~
- ~~"Level up your soldier to defeat stronger monsters!"~~

## The title, defended

**MYRIAD** — from Greek *myrias*, ten thousand. Historically a real military unit of exactly ten thousand soldiers. In modern English it means "countless."

The word literally means both *a specific finite army* and *infinity*, which is the entire game: a fixed number of soldiers, and a number that never stops going up. The title is the thesis.

**Stylization:** always uppercase, always letterspaced in display contexts — `M Y R I A D`.

**Alternate titles held in reserve** (if MYRIAD is taken on a store): *THE TEN THOUSANDTH*, *HOPLITHE*, *ANABASIS ZERO*, *STILL DYING*.
