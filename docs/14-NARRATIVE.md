# 14 — Narrative

## How story is delivered

**Fragments.** ~60 short texts, 20-90 words each, unlocked by *depth of play* — never by a quest, never blocking anything. They appear as a single line in the log (*"A fragment. #17."*) and are read in the **Archive** screen whenever the player wants. Fully skippable. Never a modal.

Unlock conditions are mechanical milestones: Reveille counts, Rank thresholds, Warden kills, Descent Layers, deaths, Ghost count, hours played.

**The rule:** a player who reads zero fragments still experiences the twist, because the twist is *mechanical*.

---

## The three acts, mapped to the prestige layers

### Act I — THE MARCH *(Reveille 1-30)*
You learn what you are: one of ten thousand, numbered, sent to kill a god, and successful. The fragments are dry — quartermaster's inventories, a march order, a casualty count that has been amended eleven times and is still wrong.

The tone is bureaucratic. The horror is administrative.

> **Fragment 4 — Casualty Return, Amended**
> *Ten thousand sent. Ten thousand returned. Ten thousand sent. The clerk has written the same line eleven times. In the margin, in a different hand: "stop."*

### Act II — THE COUNT *(Interment, Names, the Returned)*
You start noticing the enemies. The Returned wear numbers. Some of the numbers are yours. The fragments turn personal — letters, a diary, a soldier describing a march they don't remember beginning.

Around Reveille 60, the game begins **addressing you by number** in log lines. Not as a gimmick — it just starts doing it, and never comments on it.

> **Fragment 29 — Letter, Undelivered**
> *"I have been trying to work out how long. The bells are no help. I asked the man beside me his number and he gave me mine. I did not correct him. He seemed certain."*

### Act III — THE TURN *(Apotheosis)*
The reveal, delivered in three fragments and one boss fight:

1. A myriad is ten thousand soldiers.
2. A god, it turns out, is also ten thousand soldiers — that is *what a god is*, structurally. A sufficient number of the same thing, counting.
3. The Myriad did not kill a god by defeating it. They killed it by **replacing** it. And a replacement takes time to finish becoming.

The god's death is stuck because the god is *becoming you*, ten thousand times over, one Reveille at a time. That's why the numbers go up. That's why the Hollow keeps reissuing your corpses — it's not an immune system, it's **assembly**.

The final encounter, **THE MYRIAD**, is ten thousand small sigils arranged in the shape of one enormous soldier. Its statline is the literal sum of every Ghost you have ever recorded. **You fight everything you have ever been, at once.**

> **Fragment 58 — The Last Order**
> *There was never a tenth thousandth. There were nine thousand nine hundred and ninety-nine, and a space at the end of the line, and an instruction to fill it. You have been filling it. You are almost finished.*

## Why the twist is mechanically real, not just written

This is the part that matters. Every beat is a system that already exists for gameplay reasons:

| Story beat | The system that carries it | Built in |
|---|---|---|
| The Hollow reissues your dead | Ghost snapshots on every Reveille | Phase 4 |
| You fight your past selves | THE RETURNED enemy family | Phase 4 |
| You are becoming many | CHORUS class Echoes — the same Ghosts, as allies | Phase 4 |
| The count is being kept | Your soldier number in the header, always visible, never resets | Phase 0 |
| You are replacing the god | THE MYRIAD statted from the sum of all Ghosts | Phase 6 |
| The cycle continues after you | **Warden authoring** — your best Ascension becomes a boss your future runs must beat | Phase 6 |

The player who never reads a word still lives all of it. That's the design.

---

## Warden authoring (the endgame, and the last line of the story)

After the first Apotheosis, the game takes your best Ascension — class, stats, relics, Vows, sigil seed, soldier number — and saves it as a **Warden**. From then on, every subsequent Ascension encounters it at a Stand.

It is named after your soldier number. It fights exactly as you fought. Beating it authors a new one, from the Ascension that beat it.

The Hollow reissues its dead. You are the Hollow now. The loop is the ending and there isn't another one.

---

## Achievements as observations

Never "Kill 1,000 enemies." Always a statement, in voice, unlocked silently:

- *"You have died one thousand times. The Hollow has kept count. You had not asked it to."*
- *"You have played for one hundred hours. Somewhere in there, you stopped choosing to."*
- *"You reached Rank 10,000 without taking a Vow. Nobody made you do it the hard way."*
- *"You melted a True Name. It is gone. It will not roll again."*
- *"You have never once let a Stand time out."*
- *"You closed the game at Rank 1. Twice."*

---

## Things the game never explains

Leave these. They're better unresolved:

- What the god was the god *of*
- Who sent the Myriad
- Why the Hollow's records disagree by centuries
- What is on the other side of the Hollow, if there is one
- Who wrote "stop" in the margin
- Why Reveille sounds on the downstroke

---

## Ending state

There is no ending screen. After THE MYRIAD falls, the game continues — new Ascension, Rank 1, your number one higher.

The only difference is the header. It stops saying `#4,102`.

It says `#10,000`.

And it never changes again.
