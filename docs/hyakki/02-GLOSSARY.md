# 02 — The Glossary

Binding. Every renamed thing, old → new. **Never use the MYRIAD term in HYAKKI copy, and
never use a generic idle-game term in either.**

Japanese is shown with kanji on first use in-game, then romaji. No macrons in identifiers;
macrons in display text where they belong (Enma-ō, Onryō, Hyakki Yagyō).

## Voice, revised

The MYRIAD rules still hold: short sentences, second person, present tense, passive voice
allowed for what is done *to* you, no exclamation marks anywhere, ever.

**Three additions for HYAKKI:**

1. **Politeness as horror.** Much of Yomi is staffed. Oni are doing a job; the Kings are
   holding a hearing; Datsue-ba wants your coat because that is the procedure. The register
   for these is *clerical courtesy*, and it should be the most frightening thing in the game.
   > *"The King will see you. Please do not speak until the register is open."*

2. **Never explain a yōkai.** No bestiary voice, no "the karakasa is a type of…". They are
   simply present and everyone else acts as though this is normal.

3. **Japanese terms are used, not glossed.** A first-use tooltip gives the kanji and a
   four-word gloss; after that the game assumes you know. Treating the vocabulary as
   ordinary is what makes the world feel inhabited.

**Examples — good:**
- *"Ri 212 held. The thing standing in it did not."*
- *"Your tower was eleven stones. It is nothing now. Begin."*
- *"It has your walk. It is not doing it on purpose."*
- *"The umbrella has been in the family a very long time. It would like to be put down."*

**Examples — bad:**
- ~~"You defeated the Oni!"~~
- ~~"Oni are ogre-like demons from Japanese folklore."~~
- ~~"Congratulations, you have been enshrined!"~~
